Internship position

Quantum cryptography

Our ideal intern, Linda, fiddling with ordered affine partition functions.

  • Why: In recent times, quantum 1-shot signatures have received increased interest from the blockchain research community, as this esotheric cryptographic gadget is the key to achieve amazing feats such as consensus protocols with perfect finality.

    For this reason, together with other researchers we created the QSig Commission, an organization devoted to further investigate the topic. It is our goal to transform quantum 1-shot signature into a feasible piece of technology as soon as possible.

  • What: We already drafted a first implementation of an ordered affine partition hash function, which is the most fundamental theoretical advancement underlying quatum 1-shot signatures.

    The problem is that this function is not cryptographically secure at the moment, and will need to be obfuscated in some way. With our support, you will learn the basics of quantum computing and assess the feasibility of hardening ordered affine partition hash functions through obfuscation.

    Key Responsibilities:

    • Understand how ordered affine partition hash functions work. These functions are classical, so working knowledge in quantum computing is not strictly needed.
    • Investigate state of the art cryptographic obfuscation techniques, and assess their usefulness in our context.
    • Produce technical results (either theory or code) around obfuscating ordered affine partition hash functions.
